Information required for an appraisal
1. Details on Improvements/updates made to the property
• Description
• Dates implemented - Started & Completed
• Before and After Images
• Costs - Receipts
• Permits
2. Property Information
• Geological survey of the property (if provided when the property was acquired)
• Soil Reports
• Flood Information
• Septic Reports
• Blueprints/Plans/specs - floorplan (if available)
• Copy or link to the Covenants, conditions, and restrictions (CC & R) if applicable to the subdivision or community, and
• Information about the homeowner's association
3. Type of Property Single Family Vs. Condominium
4. Condominium Requirements
• Information about the homeowner's association
• Monthly fees,
• List of common elements and/or recreation facilities.
• ⁠Francie Man and Freddie Mac Requirements
• ⁠Information about special assessments (the amount for which the owner is responsible, and what the assessment covers) is also helpful.
Highly Valuable Information - Not Mandatory
5. Keepingly will find publicly listed information related to any litigation related to the
• Association,
• Contractors,
• Developers, etc.
• Which could affect value and/or marketability.
6. Actual condominium documents (if available) provided to to the appraiser or lender (If Available)
7. A link to where the documents may be recorded in the public records would be useful to the homeowner, appraiser, and lender.
